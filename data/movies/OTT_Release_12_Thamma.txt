BRIEF IDEA / STORYLINE
---

Thamma is an expansive entry into the Maddock Horror Comedy Universe, presenting a vampire tale rooted in Indian folklore and mythology. The film follows Alok, a journalist who encounters a mysterious woman named Tadaka and falls in love with her. His life takes a supernatural turn when he discovers that Tadaka belongs to a hidden world of Betaalsâ€”vampire-like creatures of ancient origin. Tadaka is bound to Yakshasan, a powerful and ancient ruler of darkness who seeks to expand his dominion over the human world. As Alok uncovers the truth about Tadaka and her origins, he finds himself caught between his love for her and the threat she and her kind pose to humanity. Together, Alok and Tadaka must band together to prevent Yakshasan from unleashing darkness upon the world. The narrative seamlessly blends supernatural horror elements with romance, drama, comedy, and heartfelt moments, creating a unique cinematic experience that hasn't been explored before in Hindi cinema.

---
AUDIENCE REVIEW
---

Thamma has been well-received as a fresh and unconventional supernatural film that successfully combines horror with humor, romance, and drama. Ayushmann Khurrana's portrayal of an ordinary man thrust into extraordinary circumstances has been praised for bringing warmth and relatability to the character. Rashmika Mandanna has been appreciated for her performance as a mystical being navigating a human world, delivering a first-of-its-kind experience in her career. Nawazuddin Siddiqui's role as the antagonist Yakshasan has generated discussion for presenting a unique take on mythological villainy. The film's scale, production design, and visual effects have been highlighted as impressive. Audiences have particularly enjoyed the film's ability to balance scares with humor, making it an entertaining watch for those who appreciate unconventional narratives. Viewers have praised the film's freshness and newness in storytelling.

---
RELEVANT INFORMATION
---

Thamma premiered on Amazon Prime Video on December 16, 2025, after its theatrical release on October 21, 2025. The film is directed by Aditya Sarpotdar, known for his work on other Maddock Horror Comedy Universe entries like Munjya. It is produced by Dinesh Vijan under Maddock Films, with Amar Kaushik as producer. The film stars Ayushmann Khurrana and Rashmika Mandanna as leads, with Nawazuddin Siddiqui as the main antagonist and Paresh Rawal in a pivotal role. Special appearances by Varun Dhawan (reprising his role from Bhediya), Nora Fatehi, and Malaika Arora enhance the film. The film is available globally on Prime Video, expanding the reach of Indian horror-comedy to international audiences.
